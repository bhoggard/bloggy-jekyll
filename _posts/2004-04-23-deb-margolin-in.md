<p>Culturebot.org has an <a href="http://www.culturebot.org/archives/2004/04/22/TalkingWithDebMargolin.php">interview</a> with the brilliant Deb Margolin.  Her wondeful book &#8220;Of All The Nerve&#8221; is really expensive, since it&#8217;s from an academic press.  Go see if <a href="http://www.powells.com/cgi-bin/partner?partner_id=26418&cgi=product&isbn=0304703192">Powell&#8217;s</a> has a used copy.</p>
<p>She will be doing work from her new &#8220;performance novel&#8221; as part of the <a href="http://www.dixonplace.org/">Dixon Place</a> <a href="http://www.theatermania.com/content/show.cfm?int_show_id=103224">Veterans Series</a> in May and early June.  I recommend going!</p>
<p>A sample:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Q: When is solo performance effective and when is it self indulgent?</p>
<p>Self-indulgence&#8230; I’m very careful about that word because I feel like that’s a word we can use to shut ourselves down. That’s the word that the inner critic uses. Sentimental that’s another one, see there is no art without sentiment. As soon as you can drop down and reveal something about your own humanity, the minute you reveal something truthful about your own humanity, you shed light on the entire spectrum of human experience. That’s why we go to the theater for the revelation, that’s why I go. I go to stare at people!</p>
<p>You can’t do it in the subway you can’t do it on a bus. You go to the theater, you pay your money and you just stare at these people. That’s what you do and no one is going to arrest you. It’s very exciting. The more specific you are with your character the more generally we see the whole human condition. And so I feel the only way to get at that could be labeled, in advance of finding the jewel of revelation, self indulgent.</p>
<p>I don’t worry about self indulgence. I don’t worry that my work is self indulgent. I feel the need to step up and take responsibility for how my work signifies politically and I feel the need to be responsible for a passionate and articulate desire to speak. Those are my responsibilities. I don’t say anything that I’m not dying to say onstage. I say the things that I cannot die without having spoken about. And you know, that’s going to reveal something weird about my humanity and in so doing it will reveal something about the entire human condition. I trust that chain of events the way I trust I’m wearing this jacket. I know that to be true I know very few things and that’s one of them. So I live my artistic life by that principle. I’m not afraid of self indulgence as long as I am passionate to speak. I know that I will be revealing something important about humanity through my own humanity. Once you find your passion for speech, and your prerogative to speak, you are unstoppable.</p>
</blockquote>
